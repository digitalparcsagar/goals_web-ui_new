Setting up your Goals.com account for the first time
📹 Walkthrough🕐 5 min read

Welcome to Goals.com. This walkthrough covers everything you need to get your workspace up and running — from the moment you create your account to the moment your first rep logs in and sees their quota on the dashboard.

The whole setup typically takes under 30 minutes. Here's what you'll do:

Step 1 — Create your workspace

When you first sign up, Goals.com creates a workspace tied to your organization. Your workspace is the central environment where all your team's data lives — pipeline, goals, commissions, and everything else.

Sign up at Goals.com

Enter your name, work email, and create a password. You'll receive a confirmation email — click the link to verify and you're in.

Name your workspace

This is usually your company name. It appears in the nav bar and in all team notifications. You can change it later in Settings.

Set your fiscal year and quota period

Goals.com needs to know when your year starts and how you measure quota (annual, quarterly, or monthly). This determines how attainment is tracked and how the leaderboard calculates pace.

Tip: If your team runs on a non-calendar fiscal year (e.g., April–March), set that now. Changing it after reps have been added will affect historical attainment data.

Step 2 — Configure your basics

Before you invite anyone, spend a few minutes setting up the core configuration so your team has a clean experience from day one.

  • Deal stages — Set up the stages in your sales process (e.g., Prospect, Qualify, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed-Won, Closed-Lost). These become the columns in your Kanban pipeline view.
  • Currency and timezone — Set these in Settings > Workspace so deal values and timestamps display correctly for your team.
  • Commission defaults — If all your reps earn the same commission rate, set the default now. You can override per-rep later.

Step 3 — Invite your team

Go to Settings > Team and click Invite Members. Enter each person's email address and assign them a role — Rep, Manager, or Admin. Each invited person gets an email with a link to set their password and join the workspace.

Note: Only Admins can invite new users, change billing settings, and configure workspace-level options. Managers can view all rep data but cannot change org-wide settings.

Step 4 — Set your first round of goals

Navigate to Goals in the left nav. Click Set Goal for each rep and enter their annual (or quarterly) quota. Once saved, each rep will see their quota and current attainment percentage on their personal dashboard the moment they log in.

Tip: Start with your current period's goals even if the period is already underway. Goals.com will calculate attainment from the deals already closed — it won't start from zero.

Step 5 — You're live

That's it. Your reps can now log in, see their quotas, add deals to the pipeline, and track their progress. As deals close, attainment updates in real time — no manual entry required.

From here, explore the rest of the Getting Started articles to learn how to import existing deal data, set up commissions, and run your first contest.

Inviting your sales team and setting up user roles
🎓 Tutorial🕐 3 min read

Goals.com has three user roles: Rep, Manager, and Admin. Each role controls what a person can see, edit, and configure. Getting roles right from the start means your team sees exactly what they need — nothing more, nothing less.

The three roles

Rep

Reps see their own pipeline, quota attainment, commissions, bonuses, and the team Highlights feed. They can add and update deals, log activities, and interact with Ace AI. They cannot see other reps' commissions or edit team-wide settings.

Manager

Managers see everything reps see, plus the full team view — all reps' pipelines, quota attainment, and commissions in one consolidated dashboard. Managers can create goals, run contests, set bonuses, and export reports. They cannot change billing, invite new users, or edit workspace settings.

Admin

Admins have full access to everything — including workspace settings, billing, user management, and all data across the organization. Assign this role carefully. Most teams have one or two Admins maximum.

How to invite someone

Go to Settings > Team

You'll see a list of existing team members and an Invite Members button in the top right.

Enter their email address and select a role

You can invite multiple people at once by adding multiple email addresses. Each gets their own role assignment.

Click Send Invite

Goals.com sends each person an email with a link to join. The link is valid for 72 hours. If it expires, resend from the Team settings page.

Set their goal once they join

After a rep accepts the invite, head to Goals and set their quota. They won't see their attainment until a goal is set.

Tip: You can change a user's role at any time from Settings > Team. Role changes take effect immediately — no need to re-invite.

Removing a team member

If someone leaves your team, go to Settings > Team, find their name, and click Deactivate. Their historical data (deals, commissions, activity) stays intact in the system — only their ability to log in is removed. This is important for accurate commission and performance records.

Important: Deactivating a user does not delete their data. If you want to permanently remove a user's data, contact Goals.com support.

A complete tour of the Goals.com platform
📚 Guide🕐 8 min read

Goals.com is a Sales Performance Software — every feature is designed around one question: Is your team winning, and what do they need to win more? This guide walks through every major section so you know what's there and why it matters.

Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first thing everyone sees when they log in. For managers, it shows the full team picture: Annual Sales Goal, Total Sales YTD, Team ROI, YTD Pace, and a live leaderboard ranked by quota attainment. For reps, it shows their personal performance — their quota, how far along they are, and where they sit on the leaderboard.

Everything on the Dashboard updates in real time. When a deal closes, the numbers move immediately — no refresh required.

Goals

The Goals section is where quota lives. Each rep has an individual goal page showing their annual or quarterly quota, current attainment as a percentage and dollar amount, YTD pace (whether they're on track to hit their number by period end), and a deal-by-deal breakdown of what's contributed to their quota so far.

When a rep's pace falls below a threshold you configure, Goals flags them as at-risk — automatically, without anyone having to check a spreadsheet.

Pipeline

Pipeline is the CRM. Every deal your team is working lives here, organized by stage. You can view it as a Kanban board (drag and drop), a List (sortable table), or a Calendar (deals and tasks by date). Deals are linked to Contacts and Accounts, so you always have the full context on who you're selling to and what else is happening with that organization.

Highlights

Highlights is the team culture layer. When a rep closes a deal, hits their monthly goal, or reaches a milestone, it posts automatically to the Highlights feed — and the whole team can react and comment. It's how Goals.com keeps wins visible and recognition organic, especially for remote or distributed teams.

Bonuses

Bonuses lets you create incentive structures on top of base commission. You define the target (close 5 deals, generate $50K in revenue, make 100 calls) and the reward, and Goals.com tracks each rep's progress automatically. Reps see a progress bar showing how close they are to unlocking each bonus.

Contests

Contests are time-boxed competitions with a live leaderboard. You set a goal (most deals closed, most revenue, most activity logged), a time frame, and a prize. The leaderboard updates in real time as deals close. Past contests are archived so you can see final standings and winners.

Commissions

Every rep has a personal commissions page showing exactly what they've earned — broken down by deal, with the commission rate applied and the dollar amount calculated. There's also a YTD total and a next payout date. Managers can see the full team view and export commission data for payroll.

Reports

Reports is the export center. You can export Opportunities, Contacts, Activities, Goals & Performance, and Commissions as CSV or XLSX files in one click. You can also schedule reports to run automatically — weekly, monthly, or on a custom cadence — and have them delivered to your inbox.

Ace AI

Ace is the built-in AI assistant. It knows your pipeline, your reps, and your history. You can ask it anything in plain English — "which deals are at risk this quarter?" or "what's our projected close for Q3?" — and it answers with real data from your account. It's available from any page in the platform via the Ace icon in the left nav.

Chat

Chat is built-in team messaging. You can create topic-specific channels (e.g., #closers, #deals, #announcements) and send direct messages to individual team members. It keeps sales communication inside the same tool where the work is happening — so context doesn't get lost across apps.

Importing your contacts and deals from another CRM
🎓 Tutorial🕐 6 min read

If you're moving to Goals.com from another CRM — Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, or a spreadsheet — the import tool lets you bring your existing contacts, accounts, and deals without starting from zero.

Goals.com accepts CSV files for all import types. If your current CRM exports in another format (XLSX, JSON), convert to CSV first using Google Sheets or Excel.

What you can import

  • Contacts — names, emails, phone numbers, titles, and linked organizations
  • Accounts — company names, industries, website, and notes
  • Opportunities (Deals) — deal name, value, stage, linked contact, linked account, close date, and assigned rep

Commission history and activity logs (calls, notes) cannot be imported — those are entered going forward inside Goals.com.

Preparing your CSV

Before importing, clean up your data. A few things that cause issues:

  • Duplicate contacts — Goals.com uses email address as the unique identifier for contacts. If the same email appears twice, only one record will import.
  • Deal stages that don't match — Your CSV deal stages need to match the stage names you configured in Goals.com exactly (case-sensitive). If your CSV says "In Negotiation" but Goals.com has "Negotiation," the deal will import into the default first stage.
  • Currency formatting — Deal values should be plain numbers (42000, not $42,000). Remove currency symbols and commas before importing.
Tip: Download the Goals.com import template from the Import page (Settings > Import > Download Template) before formatting your data. It shows exactly which column headers are expected.

Running the import

Go to Settings > Import

You'll see separate import options for Contacts, Accounts, and Opportunities. Always import in this order: Accounts first, then Contacts, then Opportunities — because deals need to be linked to contacts and accounts that already exist.

Upload your CSV file

Click Upload and select your file. Goals.com will preview the first few rows and ask you to map columns to fields.

Map your columns

Each column in your CSV needs to be matched to a Goals.com field. Required fields are marked with a red asterisk. Unmapped columns are skipped — they won't cause errors, just won't import.

Review and confirm

Goals.com shows a summary: X records will import, Y have errors (with explanations). Fix errors in your CSV and re-upload, or skip the problem rows and import the clean records now.

Check your data

After import, spot-check a few records in Pipeline and Contacts to confirm everything landed correctly. Pay particular attention to deal stages and assigned rep names.

After the import

Imported deals do not automatically count toward quota attainment unless their Close Date is within the current quota period and their status is Closed-Won. Open deals import as active pipeline — they'll count toward attainment when they're marked closed inside Goals.com.

Note: Imports cannot be undone in bulk. If you import incorrect data, you'll need to delete records individually or contact support. Always test with a small sample file first.

Finding your way around — navigating Goals.com
📚 Guide🕐 2 min read

Goals.com uses a left sidebar for all navigation. Every section of the platform is one click away from anywhere. Here's a quick reference for what lives where.

The left navigation

  • Dashboard — The home screen. Shows your team leaderboard, key metrics (YTD Sales, Team ROI, Quota Pace), and the Highlights feed preview.
  • Goals — Individual rep quota pages and team-wide goal tracking. This is where you set, view, and manage sales quotas.
  • Pipeline — Your CRM. All deals, contacts, and accounts live here. Toggle between Kanban, List, and Calendar views from the top of this page.
  • Highlights — The team recognition feed. Deal closes, badges, milestones, and reactions.
  • Chat — Internal team messaging. Channels and direct messages.
  • Bonuses — Active and past bonus structures. Reps see their own progress; managers see everyone's.
  • Contests — Active and past sales competitions with live leaderboards.
  • Commissions — Per-deal commission breakdowns, YTD totals, and payout schedules.
  • Reports — The export center. One-click data exports and scheduled report configuration.
  • Ace ✦ — The AI assistant. Available from any page.

The top bar

The top bar contains global search (search for any deal, contact, or account by name), your notification bell (alerts for at-risk deals, contest updates, and new Highlights), and your profile menu (account settings, workspace settings, and sign out).

Quick tips

  • Press / from anywhere to open global search instantly.
  • Click any rep's name on the Dashboard leaderboard to jump directly to their Goal page.
  • In Pipeline, use the My Deals filter to see only your own opportunities — useful if you're a rep looking at a busy team pipeline.
  • Ace AI is always one click away — look for the ✦ icon at the bottom of the left nav.
Tip: Bookmark your most-used section (usually Pipeline or Goals) so you land there directly each day instead of routing through the Dashboard.
